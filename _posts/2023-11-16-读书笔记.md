### The Working of Competitive Markets
- SR prices: market prices
- LR prices: natural prices
- Competition as fundamentally requiring a large number of sellers; a group of resource owners who were knowledgeble about profits, wages, and rents in the economy; and freedom of movement for resources among industries
- Given these conditions, the self-interest of resource owners would lead to the LR natural prices that would equalize the profits, rents and wages
- AS not completely advocate in laissez faire 
- He think that exceptions of tarrif on infant industries is necessary.
- Trade regulation was necessary 
- Advocating government provision of goods that have great social benefits but that are not supplied by the private market

### Capital and Capitalists
- The wealth of nation determined by capital accumulation
- Capital accumulation determines the division of labor and the proportion engaged in productive labor
- Capital accumulation leads to economic development
- Individual self-interest coupled with the accumulation of capital leads to an optimum allocation of capital among the various industries
- Capitalists plays the key role in the functioning of the economy
- Capitalists pursuit of wealth and profits directs the economy to an efficient allocation of resources and economic growth
- Source of private capital is savings 
- Labor could not accumulate capital because the level of wages permitted only the satisfaction of immediate consumption desires
- Without unequal income distribution, economic growth is not possible

### The Nature and Causes of the Wealth of Nations
- Wealth was an annual flow of goods and services, not an accumulated fund of precious metals
- His opening sentence he implied that the end purpose of economic activity is consumption
- Physiocrat: Consumption 
- Mercantilism: Production

### Causes of the Wealth of Nations
1. Productivity of labor
   - Specialization and division of labor increase labor productivity
   - The larger the market, the greater the volume that can be sold and the greater the opportunity for division of labor
   - A limited market, on the other hand, permits only limited division of labor.

2. Productive and unproductive labor
   - The accumulation of capital, according to Smith, also determines the ratio between the number of laborers who are productively employed and those who are not so employed
   -  Labor employed in producing a vendible commodity is productive labor, Smith held, whereas labor employed in producing a service is unproductive
   -  Smith insisted that the highest rates of economic growth would be achieved by distributing large incomes to the capitalists, who save and invest, and low incomes to the landlords, who spend for menial servants and “who leave nothing behind them in return for their consumption"
   -  economic growth is inhibited by government spending for unproductive labor, it is better to have less government and, lower taxes on the capitalists so that they may accumulate more capital.
  
### Summary of the Causes of the Wealth of Nations
- Capital is the chief determinant of the wealth of nations
- The larger the proportion of capital accumulation to total output, the greater the rate of economic growth
- In a system of private property, a further requirement for high rates of capital accumulation is an unequal distribution of income.

### Relative Prices
- Smith believed that SR prices are determined by both demand and supply
- Natural, or LR equilibrium prices generally depend on costs of production
- Smith’s analysis of the formation of relative prices distinguishes two time periods, the SR and the LR, and two broad sectors of the economy, agriculture and manufacturing
- During the SR period, Smith found downward-sloping demand curves and upward- sloping supply curves in both manufacturing and agriculture
- Natural price in agricultural sector depends on supply and demand because the LR supply curve is upward-sloping, indicating increasing costs
- But for the manufacturing sector, the LR supply curve is at times assumed to be perfectly elastic, representing constant costs, and in other parts of the analysis is downward-sloping, indicating decreasing costs
- In manufacturing, when LR supply curve is perfectly elastic, price depends on cost of production; but when it is downward-sloping, natural price depends upon both demand and supply.
- Determination of natural prices is on cost of production

### The Meaning of Value
- Value distinguish 
  1. Value in use
  2. Value in exchange
- Value in exchange is the power of a commodity to purchase other 
goods—its price
- Value in use is the want-satisfying power of a commodity, the utility received by holding or consuming a good
- Several kinds of utility are received when a commodity is consumed:    
  1. total utility
  2. average utility
  3. marginal utility
- Smith focus on total utility
- The price we are willing to pay for a commodity—the value we place on acquiring another unit—depends not on its total utility but on its marginal utility
